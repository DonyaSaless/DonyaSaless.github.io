Hello everyone, welcome to my website!

This is Donya, I am a senior student of both EE-Communication (Major) and CE (Minor) in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Tehran.

I am naturally curious about the world around me and very enthusiastic about math! and as one once said, "Beautiful math tends to be useful, and useful things tend to have beautiful math." It didn't take me so long to realize that understanding theoretical aspects of problems, particularly in Machine Learning, is a genuine delight to me. Along the way, I'd like to use my knowledge of signal processing, algebra, statistics, information theory, various algorithm design methods, and data structures as my primary tools.

"The beauty of mathematics only shows itself to more patient followers," and I would be lying if I said I was patient enough during my undergrad. I wanted to explore and experience as much as possible, touch different problems, get familiar with various fields, and of course, build a strong background for graduate school. I took various theoretical courses and worked as an intern in different places, from the Sharif University of Technology, Iran, to the Max Planck Institute, Germany, and the University of Texas at Austin, USA. I had the opportunity to get to know fantastic people from all around the world, work with them, and learn from them, resulting in a paper accepted in ICML 2021 Self Supervised Learning Workshop, with an extension accepted in NetSci-X 2022.

I was fortunate to work on my Bachelor's Thesis under the supervision of Prof. Keshav Pingali from the University of Texas at Austin. I am the first Bachelor student in my university who has officially done her thesis internationally.

Some people collect stamps, and some collect erasers; I had an unexpected surgery late in December, and bed rest reminded me of my own collection, some of the useful links and materials I collected along the way. I am putting them here as they might end up being helpful for friends who are walking a path similar to mine (or even different from mine! Familiarity with a broad range of subjects is always helpful for gaining intuition.)
